# AI Trends Report — 2025

## Executive Summary

The AI landscape in 2025 has been defined by three major shifts: the rise of small language models (SLMs), the mainstreaming of AI agents, and the growing importance of on-device AI inference.

## Key Findings

### 1. Small Language Models

Models under 10B parameters have become surprisingly capable for domain-specific tasks. Microsoft's Phi-3 family and Google's Gemma 2 have shown that careful data curation can compensate for smaller parameter counts. Enterprise adoption has accelerated as SLMs offer better cost-efficiency and latency.

### 2. AI Agents

Autonomous AI agents moved from research demos to production deployments. Key developments include:
- Claude Code and similar coding agents handling multi-step software engineering tasks
- Research agents capable of end-to-end literature review and synthesis
- Customer service agents with access to company knowledge bases

The shift from "chat" to "do" has been the defining trend.

### 3. On-Device Inference

Apple Intelligence, Qualcomm's NPU chips, and Google's Tensor G4 have made local AI inference practical for consumer devices. Privacy-preserving AI processing is now a competitive differentiator.

## Market Data

- Global AI market: $190B (up 35% YoY)
- Enterprise AI adoption: 72% of Fortune 500 companies
- Average AI project ROI: 3.5x within 18 months
- AI-related job postings: up 40% year-over-year

## Conclusions

The democratization of AI continues. As models become smaller and more efficient, the barrier to entry drops. The next frontier is reliable AI agents that can operate autonomously in complex environments.
